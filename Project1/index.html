Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

1630 - 1680

Founder of the Maratha Empire

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, a towering figure in Indian history, was a visionary leader and a valiant warrior who laid the foundation for the Maratha Empire in the 17th century. Born in 1630 in Shivneri Fort, Shivaji demonstrated his military prowess and administrative acumen from a young age. He believed in the principles of Swarajya (self-rule) and Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule for the Indian people). Shivaji Maharaj is best remembered for his guerrilla warfare tactics, which he employed effectively against the powerful Mughal Empire. Through his strategic brilliance and charismatic leadership, he carved out an independent kingdom in the heart of the Deccan region, challenging the dominance of the Mughals and other regional powers. Apart from his military achievements, Shivaji was also known for his administrative reforms and commitment to justice. He established a well-organized administration with efficient revenue collection systems and promoted religious tolerance. Shivaji Maharaj's legacy continues to inspire millions across India. His courage, resilience, and dedication to his people make him an enduring symbol of valor and patriotism. Even today, he is revered as one of India's greatest heroes, whose ideals of self-determination and integrity continue to resonate with generations.

Biographies

  • Raised by mother Jijabai, learned valor, righteousness.
  • Innovated guerrilla tactics, "Ganimi Kava".
  • Founded Maratha Empire, Raigad capital, 1674.
  • Introduced "Ashta Pradhan" council, promoted justice, governance
  • Respected all faiths, ensured protection.
  • Symbol of courage, patriotism, celebrated annually on Shivaji Jayanti.
